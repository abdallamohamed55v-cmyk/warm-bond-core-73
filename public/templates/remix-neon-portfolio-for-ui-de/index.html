01
Zepto Essentials — Smart Grocery Planning
Service Design UX Research Interaction Design
Tagline

"Turning repetitive grocery runs into one-tap convenience."

The Problem

Monthly grocery shopping on quick-commerce apps is repetitive and time-consuming. Users reorder the same essentials every month yet navigate the full catalog each time. There's no smart planning layer — leading to missed items, multiple orders, and decision fatigue.

My Approach

Conducted contextual inquiries with 15+ users to map monthly grocery habits. Designed a smart "Essentials Planner" feature — an AI-driven monthly basket that learns from past orders, auto-suggests replenishments, and allows one-tap reordering. Created service blueprints, user flows, and a high-fidelity prototype tested across two iterations.

-60%

Outcome

The Essentials Planner reduced average monthly reorder time by 60% in usability testing. Users described the experience as "effortless" and "finally how grocery apps should work." The service design framework also uncovered backend optimization opportunities for delivery batching.

02
Safe Choice — Safer & Simpler Decisions
UX Design Trust & Safety User Testing
Tagline

"Clear choices. Confident decisions."

The Problem

Users often face critical everyday decisions — from product safety to service reliability — without clear, trustworthy information. Existing platforms overload with reviews and ratings but lack structured clarity, leading to hesitation, second-guessing, and poor choices driven by information overload.

My Approach

Designed a trust-first decision framework with layered information architecture. Built clarity-driven UI patterns: safety scores, visual comparison cards, and confidence indicators. Conducted 12 user tests focused on decision speed and post-decision satisfaction. Iterated on hierarchy and micro-copy to maximize trust signals.

Outcome

User testing showed a 73% increase in decision confidence and 40% faster decision-making compared to baseline. Participants highlighted the safety scores and comparison cards as "game changers" for building trust. The project was recognized for its innovative approach to designing for user confidence.

03
Prayass — Empowering Through Design
End-to-End Accessibility Social Impact
Tagline

"Small efforts. Big impact."

The Problem

Underserved communities often lack access to intuitive digital tools that address their real-world challenges. Existing solutions are either too complex, not localized, or designed without empathy for the end user's constraints — from low digital literacy to limited connectivity.

My Approach

Followed an empathy-driven design process starting with field research and stakeholder interviews. Designed an accessible, multilingual interface with minimal cognitive load — large touch targets, icon-first navigation, and offline-friendly architecture. Built the full experience from research through high-fidelity prototype, validated with community testing sessions.

Outcome

Community testing achieved a 91% task completion rate among first-time digital users. The icon-first navigation and simplified flows were praised for making technology feel approachable. Prayass was selected for a social impact design showcase and recognized for its meaningful, human-centered approach.
