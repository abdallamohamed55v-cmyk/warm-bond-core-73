The surface breaks

At the boundary between air and ocean, light scatters into a thousand shards. Every wave is a lens, bending the world above into something unrecognizable. Here, the familiar dissolves.

0 – 200m
02

Twilight descends

The mesopelagic zone swallows colour whole. Red vanishes first, then orange, then yellow. What remains is an eternal blue twilight — a world painted in a single, diminishing hue.

200 – 1 000m
03

Pressure builds

In the bathypelagic, sunlight is a memory. The only illumination comes from within — bioluminescent flashes that punctuate an otherwise absolute darkness. Life here writes its own language in light.

1 000 – 4 000m
04

Silence speaks

The abyssal plain stretches like a desert of silt. Temperatures hover just above freezing. And yet, around hydrothermal vents, entire ecosystems thrive — powered not by the sun, but by the earth itself.

4 000 – 6 000m

The Hadal Zone

Below everything,
life persists.

At the deepest trenches of the ocean floor, in crushing darkness and impossible pressure, organisms endure. A reminder that resilience has no lower limit.
